The Reason I Study Computer Science

My goal in life is to become a great programmer and an even better problem-solver.

This has been something that's originated as a younger child, but I never took initiative to learn about programming because I thought that it would be too hard. I guess that my mind at the time didn't want to take on such a challenge. I knew since a child that I wanted to be some sort of an engineer, before I even knew what an engineer actually was.

Up until high school graduation, I never truly had a grasp on reality. I never knew what I wanted to do nor did I decide to take the time to think about what I wanted to do until I basically had to make a choice.

This created a time where I had to take a deep dive within myself and reflect on what it is I wanted out of life, do I want to continue wasting time when I know there's much more to life than what's distracting me?
So, I decided to finally take a shot and start pursuing in a career I've been meaning to pursue in for a very long time, Computer Science.

So far, I've been loving the process including the infamously strenuous math curriculum (though I haven't went into Calculus yet, I still have one more semester of the broken up Precalculus)
and am very excited to continue learning as I refuse to let anything get in my way of my goal. No matter the difficulty, failure is not an option.
